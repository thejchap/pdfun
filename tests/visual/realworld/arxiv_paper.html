Notes on Greedy Coloring of Sparse Graphs

Jane Q. Researcher, John P. Coauthor

Department of Theoretical Whatever, University of Somewhere

Abstract

We present a brief survey of greedy coloring strategies for sparse graphs and discuss bounds on chromatic number under natural orderings. The treatment is informal and is intended for use as a layout regression fixture only.

1. Introduction

Graph coloring assigns labels (colors) to vertices so that adjacent vertices receive different labels. The minimum number of colors required is the chromatic number, denoted χ(G).

Greedy algorithms process vertices in some order and assign each the smallest color not used by an already-colored neighbor. The number of colors used depends strongly on the chosen ordering.

2. Bounds

2.1 Trivial bound

Greedy coloring uses at most Δ(G) + 1 colors, where Δ(G) is the maximum degree.

χ(G) ≤ Δ(G) + 1.

2.2 Welsh–Powell ordering

Sorting vertices by non-increasing degree improves the bound on many natural inputs but does not change the worst-case guarantee.
